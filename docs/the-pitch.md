# Let me tell you what I'm building

You know how every transient house here runs? Messenger for the inquiries, GCash for the 30% reservation, a screenshot for "proof of payment," and a spreadsheet nobody keeps updated. So they miss messages, they double-book during peak season, and they're always half-worried about scams. Every operator lives this every single day.

Here's the thing that got me: nobody good is actually serving these people.

The big hotel software (Cloudbeds, RoomRaccoon, Little Hotelier) starts at like $150 to $200 a month and is built for 20 to 50 room hotels. Way too expensive and too heavy for a small 1 to 5 room transient house. And the closest local one, Collo, is solid but it's for long-term rent, condos, dorms, HOA dues. It doesn't do nightly bookings at all. No calendar, no booking page, no hold timer. So there's this clean, wide-open gap nobody is sitting in.

That's what I'm building for. Picture this: give every small operator the thing only big hotels have right now, their own real booking system. A page guests book on directly. A calendar that never double-books. GCash payment that kills the whole screenshot dance. And one clean board that just tells them what needs their attention right now. Take all the Messenger-and-spreadsheet chaos and turn it into something that basically runs itself. Then do that for thousands of operators across the country.

And it's not just an idea, that's the part I'm excited about. It already works. It's a real multi-tenant system: operators sign up and get verified, each one gets their own booking page, there's the calendar, the smart "needs action" board, the hold-confirm-pay flow that stops double-bookings, online GCash payments where each operator uses their own account, email notifications, an admin layer, free and paid tiers. The boring-but-hard foundation (data properly isolated, money handled safely, payments that can't get charged twice) is already done. The stuff most startups burn a year and a lot of money just trying to reach.

The money makes sense too. Free tier to get them off Messenger and in the door. Paid tier for what they'll actually pay to unlock, taking real online payments and ending the double-booking mess. Probably ₱500 to ₱1,500 a month, or a small per-booking fee. There are thousands of these operators and right now they're all basically paying for the problem instead of the solution.

So that's it. Real painful problem, a gap the big guys price themselves out of and the local guy doesn't touch, a product that already works on a serious foundation, and a market that already uses the exact payment rails this is built on. The pieces just line up. I really think this can be something.
