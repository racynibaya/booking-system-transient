# Tuloy — Social Media Strategy (Facebook & Instagram)

_Tuloy: the commission-free direct-booking tool for San Juan, La Union accommodation operators. Goal: build awareness with operators so they sign up and try it._

## The one-line goal

Get every small accommodation operator in San Juan (and the surf community around them) to know Tuloy exists and think: _"This solves the exact mess I deal with every day."_ Awareness now; signups follow.

## Who we're talking to

- **Small operators (primary):** transient house, homestay, and surf resort owners with 1–15 rooms. They run bookings on Messenger + GCash + a notebook. They double-book during peak season, chase payment screenshots, and lose inquiries.
- **Hotels (anchors):** want a commission-free _direct_ channel alongside Agoda/Booking (they bleed ~15% to OTAs).
- **The San Juan tourism community:** surf camps, co-ops, barangay tourism pages, local FB groups — multipliers who spread the word.
- _(Later: tourists, once the marketplace turns on. Not the focus yet.)_

## The core message

**"Turn your Facebook page into a real booking system. No double-bookings. No screenshot chasing. No commission."**

Supporting hooks:

- One link na lang — guests reserve and pay a deposit themselves.
- A calendar that never double-books across your rooms.
- GCash deposits straight to _your_ account. Tuloy never holds your money.
- **Zero commission, ever.** Flat subscription, free during the pilot.

## Brand voice

Founder-to-operator. Plainspoken, warm, a little cheeky. Talks like a San Juan local who's lived the problem — Taglish welcome ("Tapos na ang screenshot drama"). Confident but never corporate. We name the pain honestly, then show the fix.

Do: speak to real daily headaches, use simple words, show the product, sound human.
Don't: use startup jargon ("synergy," "leverage," "revolutionize"), overpromise, or talk down to operators.

## Content pillars (the 5 things we post about)

1. **The Pain (relatable)** — "POV: peak season and you just double-booked Room 2." Name the chaos every operator knows. Most shareable pillar.
2. **The Fix (product in action)** — Screen recordings/screenshots: the booking page, the calendar, the hold timer, the "needs action" board. Show, don't tell.
3. **Proof & Trust** — Operator quotes, before/after, "no commission" math vs OTAs, how deposits go straight to them.
4. **San Juan Pride (community)** — Surf culture, local operators, "supporting San Juan tourism." Builds belonging and reach in local groups.
5. **Calls to Action** — "Free during pilot — claim your booking page." Kept to ~20% so the feed feels useful, not salesy.

## Posting cadence

- **Facebook:** 4–5 posts/week (where operators actually are — FB groups, Marketplace, pages).
- **Instagram:** 3–4 posts/week + Stories/Reels (reach younger operators, surf community).
- **Best times (PH):** weekdays 12–1pm and 8–10pm (operators free after check-ins); Sundays late morning.
- Format per platform: square/portrait images, short screen-recording Reels.

## The 80/20 rule

80% value (relatable pain, product demos, San Juan content, tips). 20% direct ask (sign up / claim page). Operators follow tools that help them, not tools that nag.

## Growth tactics (first 90 days)

- Post (within group rules) in San Juan / La Union / transient-operator and surf FB groups — the pain posts perform best there.
- DM/tag a few friendly operators to be first "founding users" and feature them.
- 1 Reel/week: a 20–30s screen recording of a guest booking + deposit landing — the "magic moment."
- Reply to every comment and DM fast; that responsiveness _is_ the marketing.
- Partner shout-outs with surf camps and local tourism pages so content spreads to their followers.

## What success looks like (track monthly)

Page follower growth, post **shares** and saves (top awareness signals), reach in local groups, profile/link clicks to the signup page, and DMs from interested operators. Awareness first; signups are the conversion.

## A note on accuracy

Keep claims true to the current product: **no commission ever, deposits pass straight through to the operator's own GCash/gateway, free during the pilot, room-gated pricing afterward (Solo ≤4 rms ₱990 · Pro ≤15 ₱2,500 · Business unlimited ≈₱5,900, value-priced/contact-sales).** Don't promise the tourist marketplace yet — that's a later layer.
